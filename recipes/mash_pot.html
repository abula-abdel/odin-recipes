Mashed Potato Recipe

Homepage

Description

Mashed potatoes should be buttery, fluffy, and smooth with just the right amount of richness. This recipe is easy, uses common ingredients, and gives you restaurant-level results without any complicated steps. Perfect for holidays, dates, or a quick cozy meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs potatoes (Yukon Gold for creamy, Russet for fluffy — or mix both)
  • 4 tablespoons (½ stick) unsalted butter
  • ½ cup milk or heavy cream (warm it before using)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Black pepper, to taste

Steps:

Prep the Potatoes

  1. Peel potatoes (or leave some skin on if you like texture).
  2. Cut into even chunks (about 1–2 inches).
  3. Rinse once to remove excess starch.

Boil until very tender

  1. Add potatoes to a large pot and cover with cold water.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon salt to the water.
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce to medium and cook for 15–20 minutes until fork-tender.

Drain and dry

  1. Drain potatoes well.
  2. Return them to the warm pot for 1–2 minutes to let steam escape — this makes creamier mash.

Mash the potatoes

  1. Add butter first so it melts into the potatoes.
  2. Mash using a masher, whisk, or ricer.
  3. Slowly pour in warm milk/cream while mashing until you reach your desired consistency.

Season and finish

  1. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  2. Add optional sour cream or chives if using.
  3. Mix gently — don’t over-mix or they can become gluey.
